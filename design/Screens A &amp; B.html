Phase 02 · Midnight Garden · Screen A & B

Stress-test: dense profile vs. weightless capture.

Two iPhone screens designed in the Midnight Garden language. Screen A is content-rich (a real person with 7 interests, 4 saved ideas in three states, 4 history entries, notes, an AI CTA). Screen B is the share-sheet capture — autofocused, single-tap save, minimal chrome. The contrast is the point.

What I'm uncertain about

If I had another pass…

  • The "18 days" countdown is loud. The italic 44pt brass numeral feels editorial in isolation but might dominate the screen — especially when the occasion is months away. Should the size scale with urgency? Big at <30 days, smaller at 100+?
  • Gift status as 3 dots-and-pills. "Idea / Shortlist / Ordered" — three states might be too few (no "given," no "rejected") or too many for the capture flow. Worth interviewing real users on what stages they actually track.
  • Capture's "From clipboard" card assumes pasted URLs auto-detect. That's a lot of magic — if it misfires it's annoying. Might need to be explicit: a "paste link" button rather than ambient detection.
What I want your feedback on

Specifically:

  • AI CTA prominence. I've made "Help me find a gift" the only brass-filled button on the profile, with a soft glow. Is that the right weight, or should AI assist be quieter (a chip near interests) so it doesn't define the app?
  • Type density on the profile. Eyebrow labels (mono, ALL CAPS) carry a lot of structural weight. On a real phone this might feel busy. Should some sections drop the label and rely on visual grouping?
  • Capture as a sheet vs. full screen. Right now Screen B looks like a fullscreen modal. If it's a share-sheet bottom-sheet, the headline "What did you think of?" might be too tall — every pixel above the fold matters. Want to validate this before mocking the share-sheet entry.
